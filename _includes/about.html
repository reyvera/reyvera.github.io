About

     From the beautiful, and sunny state of California, only to be raised in Tulare county (think dairy cows around every corner). Since agriculture was not a way of life for my family, I knew my educational, and ultimately occupational, choices needed be made based on the ability to adapt to any environement I may find myself in.

     As a child, I was enamored with the process of problem solving, whether that be through playing with legos, or taking apart my mother's blender. Today, I can honestly admit that I cared more about wanting to know how "things" work, rather than how to put it back together. This determined the trajectory for the majority of my education experience from grade-school, through univerisity. It has since become easier to admit my infatuation with science, math, and history!

     This, of course, ultimately lead to undergrad studies at CSU Fresno (Go Bulldogs!) in Engineering, and Mathematics (with a minor in philosophy). During this time, I paid some of my tuition through ethical hacking, web development, and tutoring others in various subjects. I managed to take full advantage of what the university had to offer, to wit, sitting-in on lectures I wasn't enrolled in, thereby leading to a greater understanding in advanced physics, and computer science.

     Throughout the years, I have also found myself enjoying the process of making music (in a literal sense). Spending time mastering the bass guitar, then moving on to the electric/acoustic guitar, it wasn't long until I began to adapt my curious nature towards this love of music. This began the journey of designing, and constructing electric guitars, and various analog/digital effect pedals.

     Of course, the journey doesn't stop there! I find myself constantly in a state of searching for the next big project, or exploring uncharted depths of knowledge. Looking forward to what the future has in store!
